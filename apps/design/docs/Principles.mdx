# Principles

From `docs/brand/aesthetic.md`. These are the commitments every surface is graded against.

### 1. Lightweight

> No design that weighs the page down. Prefer white space to ornament, borders to blocks, structure to surface.

### 2. Translucent

> Surfaces breathe. Float, not stack. Glass panels where depth adds meaning; plain cards where it doesn't.

### 3. Quiet by default

> We don't dance for the user. Motion is purposeful; color is reserved for signal. Loud is a tool, not a default.

### 4. Editorial

> Fraunces for display, Geist for body, JetBrains for code. Tight leading, wide tracking on labels, plenty of white space between sections.

### 5. Purposeful motion

> Three kinds only: entrance (`fade-in`, `pop-in`, `stagger-in`), feedback (`press-scale`, `pulse-dot`), loading (`shimmer`, `typing-dot`). Everything else is decoration and gets cut.

### 6. Two rooms, one world

> The product app and the marketing site are two rooms in the same building. Same palette, same type scale, same motion curves — different furniture.

## Voice

From `docs/brand/foundation.md` §10: **honest, terse, technical-without-bravado**.

- Use a period at the end of UI microcopy.
- "Workspace" not "organization". "Commessa" not "project code".
- No emoji in product UI. They're fine in marketing.
- Error copy states what happened, not what's wrong. "Couldn't sign you in" beats "Error 403".

## Anti-patterns

These exist in the codebase today and are scheduled for removal (see `docs/brand/aesthetic.md` §2 — "Seven concrete moves").

- 7 role themes collapsing to 2 (light + dark). The role tint becomes an accent token per user.
- Loud entrance animations (scale from 0.5, bounce-back). Kept: the `fade-in` / `pop-in` pair. Retired: everything more theatrical.
- Over-saturated "NEW" / "Labs" badges in dense surfaces. Quiet variants preferred unless the badge is the hero of its screen.
