profile that already looks consistent with the company page.

## 3. Don't

- Don't post the exact same reshare caption from both accounts. Davide
  leads with "why I personally posted this"; Niccolò leads with the
  technical angle. Two different ledes, one carousel attached.
- Don't tag each other in the reshare captions — LinkedIn flags
  cross-account coordination and throttles the reach.
- Don't put the website link (`pulsehr.it`) in the headline slot that
  should hold a role description. The headline works for SEO and
  matching; the link goes in the contact info + About.
- Don't change your profile photo today. If your current one doesn't
  match the Pulse aesthetic, fine — that's a next-week problem, not a
  launch-day one.

## 4. Optional — at 17:10, ping five people (not mass-connect)

Five DMs, not 500 connection requests. One line each:

```
Hey — we just published the first carousel on the Pulse HR company page
(linkedin.com/company/pulse-hr-official). If you've got 60 seconds, a comment
on the post would mean a lot today. Don't upvote for its own sake —
genuinely react. Thanks.
```

Pick five people who:
- You've worked with in the last 2 years, AND
- Would plausibly find this interesting (not "everyone I've ever met"), AND
- Are the type to leave a real comment rather than a like-only reaction.

The first-hour algorithm feedback you get from five real comments is
worth more than 50 "great post!" likes.
