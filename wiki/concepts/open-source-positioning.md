# Open Source Positioning

Pulse HR is built in public, transparent on every dimension, and explicit about the trade-offs of open-source HR.

## The pillars

- **Open** — full GitHub source. No feature gates behind a sales call. License is FSL-1.1-MIT, converting to MIT after two years.
- **Transparent** — public roadmap, public changelog, public pricing, public commits, security policy in the open, telemetry schema documented.
- **Yours** — self-host on Docker / Helm / Terraform. Export everything anytime. No proprietary lock-in.
- **Built by the people who use it** — the maintainers run Pulse HR daily. The roadmap is shaped by pull requests, not product managers.

## Audience

Agencies, consultancies, and startups in the 50–500 headcount range that bill against [[Commessa]] codes and want to inspect / fork their HR tooling. Multi-country, GDPR-friendly, EU residency available.

## Why it matters here

This positioning shapes how the product behaves: [[Demo Mode]] (no login wall), [[Feedback]] (public roadmap), [[Modules]] (granular feature toggles, not paid tiers), [[Marketplace]] (export-friendly integrations).
