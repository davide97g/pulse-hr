# Vision

> An HR product the team actually opens — because it gives them something, not because HR forces them to.

Most HR tools are a tax. People log in once a quarter, fill in a form, and leave. The dashboard nobody reads. The survey nobody finishes. The 1:1 template that's secretly a stand-up replacement.

The vision for Pulse HR is the opposite: a tool the team opens **daily**, on their own, because it's where the good parts of work live — the kudos they didn't expect, the streak they're proud of, the moment a colleague's anniversary scrolls past. The same surfaces that make the day feel lighter also give HR and managers the only signal they actually need: are people okay, are they growing, are they being recognised.

If we get this right, Pulse becomes invisible infrastructure for being a kind, attentive workplace — open-source, self-hostable, owned by the team that runs on it.

## What "winning" looks like

- An engineer opens Pulse during the workday because they want to see the kudos feed, not because compliance forces them.
- A manager spends 90 seconds a week reading the recap and learns more about their team than from an hour-long 1:1.
- An HR partner replaces the annual engagement survey with the continuous Pulse vibe and recovers a month of their year.
- A founder runs the workspace on their own infra under the FSL licence and never has to "talk to sales."
