# Vision

> The tool the team installs before HR notices.

Every IC at a tech company has a Notion page, a Slack channel, and a screenshot folder where their best work goes to die. Performance review week, they screenshot the screenshots into a doc and hope. Pulse HR replaces that ritual.

The win condition is bottoms-up. One developer self-hosts the demo in 90 seconds. They like it. Their teammate gets a kudos and asks what the app is. Their team is on it by week two. Their manager opens the Friday recap and finally knows what their team did this week — without anyone filling out a form. Six months in, the company that "officially" rolls out HR tooling finds the choice was already made: keep Pulse, the team will riot if you take it away.

## What winning looks like

- A backend dev clones the repo, runs `docker compose up`, and is logging their work in two minutes.
- That dev's whole team is on it within a week. No rollout email. No training session.
- A manager reads the manager-safe recap on Friday and learns more about their team than from an hour-long 1:1.
- Performance review week, an IC walks in with a year of artefacts — status entries, kudos, achievements — and the conversation is about the work, not about remembering the work.
- Every kudos, every status, every growth note is in the team's own database, on their own infra, under a licence that converts to MIT in two years.
- A founder runs the workspace on their own server and never has to "talk to sales."
