# Employee

> **The primary persona.** Everything in Pulse HR is designed for them first; every other persona enters the product through them.

The IC — individual contributor — at a tech-forward company. A backend dev, a frontend dev, a designer, a data analyst, sometimes a PM. Mid-20s to mid-30s. Comfortable on the command line. Has opinions about their editor. Has installed at least one open-source tool this month without asking permission.

For the full positioning — how they find Pulse, how they bring their team in — see [[Target Audience]].

## What they want

- A place to log what they did today, in 30 seconds, that survives Slack scrollback.
- Recognition they can point to in six months, not a screenshot that scrolls.
- A growth trail with artefacts — kudos, achievements, status entries — they can carry into their next conversation.
- An app that respects their time: keyboard shortcuts, no modal popovers, no "Hi {firstName}" newsletter copy.
- Their data, on their infra, under a licence they can read.

## What they do daily in Pulse

- Open [[Dashboard]] for their personal snapshot — status streak, kudos this week, growth score, upcoming [[Moments]].
- Post to [[Status Log]] in the morning — three lines about what they did, what they're doing, what's blocking. ⌘⏎ to publish. The team feed is public; the per-employee sentiment recap is privacy-bounded.
- Tap their **Workload check-in** on Friday (light / balanced / heavy / overloaded). One tap, no form. See [[Saturation]].
- Send and receive [[Kudo]]s with a one-liner reason. Confetti on send.
- Log a day off in the [[Leave]] journal when they take one — no approval to wait on.
- Browse [[Growth]] for active challenges, skill paths, and their score trend.
- See [[Moments]] roll past: birthdays, anniversaries, the kudos ticker.

## How they got here

They found Pulse via a friend's Slack, a Show HN post, or a tweet with a three-line screenshot. They cloned the repo, ran `docker compose up`, and were logging their work two minutes later. They invited a teammate. The teammate stayed. That's the entire acquisition funnel.

## Features they touch

[[Dashboard]] · [[Status Log]] · [[Growth]] · [[Kudos]] · [[Moments]] · [[Saturation]] (Workload check-in) · [[Leave]] · [[Pulse]] · [[Employees]] · [[Org Chart]] · [[In-App Docs]]

Not visible to them by default (parked or admin-only): [[Time Tracking]], [[Projects]], [[Recruiting]], [[Documents]], [[Reports]] / People Insights aggregate views, [[Developers]], [[Marketplace]], [[Settings]] (except their own profile).

## Pain points addressed

- **"My best work is buried in a Slack thread from March."** → [[Status Log]] gives every working day a permanent, searchable artefact. Three lines, ⌘⏎.
- **"I don't know if my manager actually noticed the demo went well."** → [[Kudos]] makes recognition one tap; the team feed surfaces it; the artefact survives.
- **"Performance review week, I'm reconstructing my year."** → [[Growth]] keeps a continuous trail — achievements, kudos volume, skill path progress, status entries. You walk in with proof, not screenshots.
- **"Status meetings are a tax I pay for nothing."** → [[Status Log]] replaces the standup. The manager reads the recap; you keep the hour.
- **"Tracking my time off across spreadsheets is annoying."** → [[Leave]] is a personal journal — log it, see your balance, move on.
- **"Nobody asks how I'm actually doing."** → Friday Workload check-in is one tap; the manager sees the aggregate band, not the individual answer.
