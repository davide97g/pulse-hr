# Employee

The default persona. Anyone on the company roster who isn't managing people, running HR, finance, or admin. Engineers, designers, salespeople, analysts.

## Theme

Light or dark — see [[Role Themes]]. The five-persona accent palette earlier wikis described is not currently shipped; the persona shift you feel as an Employee comes from the sidebar and feature gates, not the colour.

## What they do daily in Pulse

- Open [[Dashboard]] for their personal snapshot — status streak, kudos this week, growth score, upcoming [[Moments]].
- Post to [[Status Log]] in the morning — three lines about what they did, what they're doing, what's blocking — using one of the topic presets (status / win / pain / challenge / feedback). The feed is public to the team; the per-employee sentiment recap is privacy-bounded.
- Tap their **Workload check-in** on Friday (light / balanced / heavy / overloaded). One tap, no form. See `wiki/features/saturation.md`.
- Send and receive [[Kudo]]s with a one-liner reason. Confetti on send.
- Log a day off in the [[Leave]] journal when they take one — no approval to wait on.
- Browse [[Growth]] for active challenges, skill paths and their score trend.
- See [[Moments]] roll past: birthdays, anniversaries, the kudos ticker.
- Browse the [[Org Chart]] to find a colleague.

## Features they touch

[[Dashboard]] · [[Status Log]] · [[Growth]] · [[Kudos]] · [[Moments]] · `Workload check-in` · [[Leave]] · [[Pulse]] · [[Employees]] · [[Org Chart]] · [[In-App Docs]]

Not visible to them by default (parked or admin-only): [[Time Tracking]], [[Projects]], [[Recruiting]], [[Documents]], [[Reports]] / People Insights aggregate views, [[Developers]], [[Marketplace]], [[Settings]] (except their own profile).

## Pain points addressed

- "I don't know if my manager actually noticed the demo went well." → [[Kudos]] makes recognition one tap. The team feed surfaces it.
- "Status updates are either a daily call I dread or a paragraph I never write." → [[Status Log]] is three lines, async, ⌘⏎ to publish.
- "Nobody asks how I'm actually doing." → Friday Workload check-in is one tap; the manager sees the aggregate, not the answer.
- "Tracking my time off across spreadsheets is annoying." → [[Leave]] is a personal journal — log it, see your balance, move on.
- "I want to recognize a teammate without making it weird." → kudos is one click.
